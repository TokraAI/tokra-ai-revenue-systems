# Revenue Leak Audit Scorecard

The Revenue Leak Audit Scorecard is a Tokra AI framework for diagnosing where a business is losing revenue between demand, follow-up, intake, quotes, CRM discipline, after-hours capture, and owner visibility.

Most businesses do not need more random AI tools.

They need a clearer view of where revenue is escaping.

This scorecard helps identify which leaks are costing the business the most attention, speed, trust, and sales opportunity.

## How to use this scorecard

Score each area from 1 to 5.

1 = broken or mostly unmanaged
2 = inconsistent and mostly manual
3 = functional but weak
4 = mostly reliable with some gaps
5 = clean, tracked, and systemized

The goal is not to create a perfect score.

The goal is to find the highest-value leak to fix first.

## 1. Missed demand

Question:

Is every inbound opportunity captured, logged, and followed up on?

Look for:

* Missed calls
* Unanswered forms
* Ignored emails
* Missed social DMs
* Untracked referrals
* Voicemails with no callback
* Leads stuck in disconnected inboxes

Score:

1 = inbound opportunities regularly disappear
3 = most are captured, but gaps exist
5 = every lead source is monitored, logged, and routed

System opportunity:

* Missed-call text-back
* Unified lead inbox
* Voicemail transcription
* Form capture
* Lead routing alerts

## 2. Speed to lead

Question:

How fast does the business respond when buyer intent is active?

Look for:

* Slow callbacks
* Form fills answered tomorrow
* Sales reps checking CRM manually
* No response-time tracking
* No escalation if nobody responds

Score:

1 = response time is slow and unmanaged
3 = response is decent but inconsistent
5 = response is fast, tracked, and escalated when needed

System opportunity:

* Instant acknowledgment
* Rep alerts
* SMS and email response
* Booking links
* No-response escalation

## 3. Intake quality

Question:

Does the business collect enough information to route and prioritize the lead?

Look for:

* Name and phone number only
* Missing urgency
* Missing location
* Missing job type
* Missing timeline
* Missing decision-maker status
* No clear next step

Score:

1 = intake is loose and incomplete
3 = basic intake exists but handoffs are weak
5 = intake is structured, complete, and useful

System opportunity:

* Structured intake scripts
* Required fields
* AI-assisted qualification
* Call summaries
* CRM record creation

## 4. Quote follow-up

Question:

Are quotes tracked, followed up on, and visible to the owner?

Look for:

* Quotes sent late
* Quotes sent with no follow-up
* No quote aging report
* No stale quote alerts
* No close reasons
* No visibility into open quoted revenue

Score:

1 = quote follow-up depends on memory
3 = follow-up happens sometimes but lacks discipline
5 = every quote has status, owner, follow-up, and outcome tracking

System opportunity:

* Quote follow-up reminders
* Proposal tracking
* Stale quote alerts
* Follow-up sequences
* Quote aging dashboard

## 5. Follow-up discipline

Question:

Does every interested lead have a next action?

Look for:

* “Call me next week” with no task
* No follow-up cadence
* No reminders
* No-response leads ignored
* Follow-up buried in inboxes
* Sales pipeline aging without action

Score:

1 = follow-up is mostly manual and unreliable
3 = some follow-up process exists but is inconsistent
5 = follow-up is tracked, assigned, and automated where appropriate

System opportunity:

* Follow-up cadences
* Call tasks
* SMS and email drafts
* No-response workflows
* Human approval steps

## 6. CRM hygiene

Question:

Can the team trust the CRM as the source of truth?

Look for:

* Missing lead source
* Wrong deal stage
* Duplicate records
* Missing next step
* No follow-up owner
* No quote status
* Stale opportunities

Score:

1 = CRM is messy or ignored
3 = CRM is usable but incomplete
5 = CRM is clean, current, and enforced

System opportunity:

* Missing-field alerts
* Duplicate detection
* Stage update reminders
* Call summary logging
* Stale deal reports

## 7. After-hours capture

Question:

What happens when demand comes in after closing time?

Look for:

* Night calls going to voicemail
* Weekend form fills ignored
* No emergency routing
* No next-day callback queue
* No after-hours lead report
* No owner alert for high-value opportunities

Score:

1 = after-hours demand mostly waits or disappears
3 = some capture exists but routing is weak
5 = after-hours demand is captured, categorized, and routed

System opportunity:

* 24/7 capture
* After-hours SMS response
* Emergency routing
* Appointment request intake
* Next-day callback queue

## 8. Admin drag

Question:

Is manual admin work slowing down revenue activity?

Look for:

* Manual CRM updates
* Manual notes
* Repetitive emails
* Copying data between tools
* Manual reporting
* Repeated handoff summaries

Score:

1 = admin work blocks sales and service speed
3 = some admin is managed but still heavy
5 = repetitive admin is reduced, templated, or automated

System opportunity:

* Call summaries
* Email drafts
* CRM updates
* Meeting recaps
* Job notes
* Manager reports

## 9. Owner visibility

Question:

Can the owner see where revenue is stuck?

Look for:

* No missed-call report
* No response-time report
* No quote aging
* No follow-up status
* No CRM hygiene score
* No after-hours report
* No revenue leak dashboard

Score:

1 = owner is mostly guessing
3 = owner sees some data but not enough to act fast
5 = owner has clear visibility into leaks, follow-up, and stuck revenue

System opportunity:

* Owner dashboard
* Revenue leak score
* Missed-call tracking
* Speed-to-lead tracking
* Quote aging
* Follow-up status
* CRM hygiene view

## Scoring summary

Add the scores across all nine areas.

Maximum score: 45

### 9 to 18

High leakage risk.

The business likely has major revenue escaping through missed demand, slow response, weak follow-up, or poor visibility.

Priority:

Fix the biggest leak first.

### 19 to 30

Moderate leakage risk.

The business has some process, but gaps are still costing speed, clarity, and opportunity.

Priority:

Systemize the highest-value manual bottleneck.

### 31 to 40

Low to moderate leakage risk.

The business has a functional operating layer, but specific weak points can still be improved.

Priority:

Improve visibility, automation, and consistency.

### 41 to 45

Strong system discipline.

The business likely has clean process, strong tracking, and good visibility.

Priority:

Optimize, measure, and scale.

## Audit output

A completed Revenue Leak Audit should identify:

* Highest-risk leak
* Estimated business impact
* Current workflow
* Failure points
* Recommended system fix
* Tools involved
* Human approval points
* Metrics to track
* First implementation step

## Tokra principle

The point of the audit is not to sell AI.

The point is to find where revenue is already trying to enter the business, then identify the system gap that lets it escape.

Start with the leak.

Then build the system.

Find Leaks. Fix Follow-Up. Scale Cleaner.
