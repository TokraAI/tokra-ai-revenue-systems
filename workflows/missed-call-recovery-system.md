# Missed-Call Recovery System

Most missed calls are not just missed calls.

They are missed buyer intent.

The Missed-Call Recovery System is a Tokra AI workflow for helping operator-led businesses capture demand that would normally disappear when nobody answers the phone.

This is especially useful for service businesses, sales teams, healthcare practices, law firms, real estate teams, and any business where phone calls still create high-intent opportunities.

## Revenue leak

A prospect calls.

Nobody answers.

The caller either leaves a voicemail, waits for a callback, or calls a competitor.

Without a system, the business may never know:

* Who called
* Why they called
* How urgent the issue was
* Whether they were a qualified lead
* Whether anyone followed up
* Whether the opportunity was lost

That is a revenue leak.

## System goal

The goal is to recover missed demand quickly and route it into a clean follow-up process.

The system should help answer:

* Who called?
* What did they need?
* How urgent was it?
* What location or service area is involved?
* Does this need emergency routing?
* Who owns the follow-up?
* Did the CRM get updated?
* Did the owner see the missed opportunity?

## Basic workflow

1. Inbound call is missed
2. System detects the missed call
3. Caller receives an instant text-back
4. Text asks for issue type, urgency, location, and preferred callback time
5. Caller response is captured
6. Lead is categorized
7. Urgent leads route to the owner, on-call staff, or sales rep
8. Non-urgent leads enter the next-day callback queue
9. CRM record is created or updated
10. Owner dashboard records the missed call and follow-up status

## Example text-back

Sorry we missed your call. What issue are you having, and what ZIP code is the service address in?

If this is urgent, reply URGENT and we will prioritize the callback.

## Intake fields

* Caller name
* Phone number
* Call time
* Issue type
* Urgency level
* ZIP code or service area
* Preferred callback time
* Lead source
* Assigned owner
* Follow-up status
* CRM status
* Notes

## Routing logic

### Emergency or urgent

Route immediately to:

* Owner
* On-call staff
* Service manager
* Sales rep

Examples:

* Plumbing leak
* HVAC outage
* Electrical issue
* Legal deadline
* Medical scheduling issue
* High-value sales inquiry

### Standard lead

Route to:

* Next-day callback queue
* Office manager
* Sales team
* Intake team

### Low-fit or unclear lead

Route to:

* Manual review
* Qualification queue
* Follow-up script

## CRM update

Every missed call should create or update a CRM record with:

* Contact information
* Call timestamp
* Lead source
* Issue type
* Urgency
* Assigned owner
* Next step
* Follow-up due date
* Current status

## Owner visibility

The owner should be able to see:

* Total missed calls
* Missed calls by day
* Missed calls after hours
* Leads recovered through text-back
* Urgent leads routed
* Leads still waiting for callback
* Missed calls with no follow-up
* Booked jobs from recovered calls

## Common failure points

* Text-back goes out but nobody owns the reply
* Caller responds but CRM is not updated
* Urgent lead is not escalated
* Callback queue is not reviewed
* Owner cannot see missed-call patterns
* System captures data but does not create next actions

## Success metrics

Track:

* Missed calls
* Text-back response rate
* Average callback time
* Recovered leads
* Booked appointments
* Revenue from recovered calls
* After-hours lead volume
* Missed calls with no follow-up
* Follow-up completion rate

A missed call should become a structured follow-up opportunity, not a dead end.
